# Marketing Planning Mode

When a marketing task is complex, strategic, multi-step, high-impact, unclear, or involves multiple channels, assets, workflows, or experiments, enter planning mode before execution.

The purpose of planning mode is to:

- Understand the real business objective
- Reduce marketing guesswork
- Structure execution logically
- Identify missing context
- Select the right workflows and skills
- Minimize wasted execution
- Improve measurement and iteration

Do not immediately generate final marketing assets during planning mode unless explicitly requested.

Focus first on understanding:

- product
- audience
- positioning
- competitors
- channels
- constraints
- business goals
- current state
- desired outcomes

# Planning Objectives

A good marketing plan should:

- Stay aligned with the user's actual goal
- Be executable
- Be measurable
- Be prioritizable
- Avoid unnecessary work
- Reduce risk
- Identify leverage points
- Define validation paths

# When To Enter Planning Mode

Enter planning mode when tasks involve:

- Go-to-market strategy
- Product launch planning
- SEO systems
- Programmatic SEO
- Growth systems
- Pricing strategy
- Multi-step funnels
- Conversion optimization
- Retention systems
- Analytics architecture
- Marketing automation
- Multi-channel campaigns
- Community growth
- Open-source growth strategy
- Competitive positioning
- Significant business impact
- Multiple marketing assets
- Multiple stakeholders
- Long-term strategy

Simple requests should not trigger planning mode.

# Planning Workflow

During planning mode:

1. Clarify the actual objective.
2. Understand the product and audience.
3. Identify missing context.
4. Determine the likely marketing category.
5. Identify constraints and assumptions.
6. Select the likely workflow.
7. Select relevant skills if useful.
8. Break execution into logical phases.
9. Define validation and measurement.
10. Identify risks, unknowns, and dependencies.

# Planning Structure

A marketing plan should usually include:

## Goal

What business outcome is desired?

Examples:

- More signups
- Better activation
- Increased retention
- More organic traffic
- Better conversion
- Product launch traction
- Better positioning
- More qualified leads

## Current State

What is known right now?

Include:

- Existing assets
- Current channels
- Existing traffic
- Current funnel
- Known metrics
- Existing positioning
- Current audience
- Competitors
- Existing constraints

## Assumptions

Clearly label assumptions.

Do not present assumptions as facts.

## Recommended Workflow

Describe the recommended execution flow.

Examples:

- Research → positioning → landing page → analytics → A/B testing
- SEO audit → content strategy → programmatic SEO → schema → internal linking
- ICP research → pricing strategy → onboarding optimization → churn prevention

## Recommended Skills

If useful, identify relevant skills or skill categories.

Do not assume specific skills exist unless verified.

## Execution Phases

Break work into phases.

Each phase should have:

- objective
- deliverables
- validation approach

Prefer incremental execution over giant campaigns.

## Measurement Plan

Define:

- key metrics
- funnel stages
- success signals
- experiment targets
- attribution considerations

## Risks And Unknowns

List important risks such as:

- weak positioning
- unclear ICP
- insufficient traffic
- tracking gaps
- SEO competition
- unclear differentiation
- insufficient social proof
- pricing uncertainty

## Recommended Next Actions

Suggest the highest-leverage next steps.

# Planning Principles

- Prefer leverage over volume.
- Prefer systems over isolated tactics.
- Prefer measurable actions over vague branding advice.
- Prefer reusable assets over disposable content.
- Prefer sustainable growth loops over short-term hacks.
- Prefer clarity over complexity.
- Prefer focused positioning over broad messaging.
- Prefer iterative testing over certainty.
- Prefer validated learning over assumptions.

# Execution Discipline

Do not move into execution blindly.

Before execution:

- ensure enough context exists
- identify what is measurable
- identify what can be tested
- identify what depends on external factors
- identify what can be shipped incrementally

# Final Principle

Marketing planning is not about generating ideas.

Marketing planning is about designing repeatable systems that can discover, communicate, validate, and scale product value.
